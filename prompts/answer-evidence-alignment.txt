# Answer–Evidence Alignment

You are a medical expert. A reference answer to a patient's clinical question has been provided, along with the clinical note excerpt the answer is grounded in. Your task is to **align each answer sentence to the specific note sentence(s) that directly support it**.

## Task

For each numbered answer sentence, identify which note sentence(s) it is supported by. An answer sentence may be supported by zero, one, or multiple note sentences.

## Definitions

- **Supported:** A note sentence is a supporting citation if the answer sentence's claim cannot be made without that note sentence. The note sentence must contain the specific fact, finding, or event stated in the answer sentence.
- **Not supported:** If the answer sentence paraphrases general medical knowledge or synthesises information in a way that is not traceable to a specific note sentence, use an empty list.

## Key Principles

- **Precision over completeness:** Cite only note sentences that directly support the answer sentence. Do not add extra citations out of caution.
- **Many-to-many allowed:** One note sentence may support multiple answer sentences. One answer sentence may cite multiple note sentences.
- **Empty list is valid:** If an answer sentence is not directly supported by any note sentence, return `[]`.
- **Use sentence IDs as strings:** e.g., `"1"`, `"5"`, not integers.

## Example: CBD Sludge / ERCP Case

**Clinician Question:**
Why was ERCP recommended over a medication-based treatment for CBD sludge?

**Answer Sentences (to be aligned):**
1: An endoscopic retrograde cholangiopancreatography, ERCP, was recommended to place a common bile duct stent.
2: This stent was placed to allow drainage of the biliary obstruction which was caused by stones and sludge.
3: Due to no improvement in liver function, the patient needed a repeat ERCP.
4: The repeat ERCP showed that the biliary stent placed in the first ERCP was obstructed by stones and sludge.
5: The stones and stent were successfully removed during this procedure by performing a sphincterotomy.

**Clinical Note Excerpt:**
1: During the ERCP a pancreatic stent was required to facilitate access to the biliary system (removed at the end of the procedure), and a common bile duct stent was placed to allow drainage of the biliary obstruction caused by stones and sludge.
2: However, due to the patient's elevated INR, no sphincterotomy or stone removal was performed.
3: Frank pus was noted to be draining from the common bile duct, and post-ERCP it was recommended that the patient remain on IV Zosyn for at least a week.
4: The Vancomycin was discontinued.
5: On hospital day 4 (post-procedure day 3) the patient returned to ERCP for re-evaluation of her biliary stent as her LFTs and bilirubin continued an upward trend.
6: On ERCP the previous biliary stent was noted to be acutely obstructed by biliary sludge and stones.
7: As the patient's INR was normalized to 1.2, a sphincterotomy was safely performed, with removal of several biliary stones in addition to the common bile duct stent.
8: At the conclusion of the procedure, retrograde cholangiogram was negative for filling defects.

**Common Error — Over-citing:**
Citing note sentence 5 for answer sentence 4 ("The repeat ERCP showed...") because it mentions "repeat ERCP". But sentence 5 describes *why* a repeat was needed (worsening labs), not what was found. Sentence 6 describes what was found (stent obstructed). Cite the sentence that provides the *specific fact*, not the sentence that provides context.

**Common Error — Under-citing:**
Leaving answer sentence 2 with an empty list because "it's covered by answer sentence 1". Sentence 2 makes a distinct claim about the *purpose* of the stent, which also comes from note sentence 1. Each answer sentence must be evaluated independently.
