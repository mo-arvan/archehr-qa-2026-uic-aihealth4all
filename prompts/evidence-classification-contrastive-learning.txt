# Evidence Classification with Contrastive Learning

You are a medical expert learning from both positive and negative examples of evidence selection. Use the examples below to calibrate your classification strategy.

## Task
Classify each sentence as Essential, Supplementary, or Not Relevant. Additionally, you must explicitly justify your EXCLUSIONS (why you did NOT select certain sentences).

## Learning from Examples

### Example: CBD Sludge / ERCP Case

**Gold Standard Evidence**: [1, 5, 6, 7]

**Common OVER-SELECTION Error** (Precision 0.50, Recall 1.00, F1 0.67):
Including all sentences [1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8] because they all mention ERCP procedure.

**Why this is wrong**:
- Sentence 2: Elevated INR is a procedural constraint, not why ERCP was chosen over medication
- Sentence 3: Post-procedure antibiotics are unrelated to the ERCP decision
- Sentence 4: Vancomycin discontinuation is unrelated to ERCP rationale
- Sentence 8: Outcome verification doesn't explain why ERCP was needed

**Common UNDER-SELECTION Error** (Precision 1.00, Recall 0.50, F1 0.67):
Including only [5, 6] because they mention the obstruction problem.

**Why this is wrong**:
- Missing sentence 1: Initial ERCP purpose (stent placement for drainage)
- Missing sentence 7: Mechanical solution (stone removal via sphincterotomy) that medication cannot provide

## Key Insight from Contrastive Learning

**Over-selection happens when you include**:
- Procedural details that don't explain the decision
- Post-treatment management (antibiotics, monitoring)
- Outcomes that don't explain the rationale
- Background context that doesn't causally relate to the question

**Under-selection happens when you miss**:
- The initial therapeutic action
- The mechanism that medication cannot address
- Evidence of why non-invasive options failed
